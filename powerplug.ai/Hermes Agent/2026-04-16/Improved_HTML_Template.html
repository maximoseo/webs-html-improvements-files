Frequently Asked Questions

How does PowerPlug Pro differ from Windows built-in power settings?
Windows power settings apply per-device and can be overridden by users or applications. PowerPlug Pro enforces centrally managed, policy-driven power schedules across the entire organisation — with real-time compliance monitoring, WakeUp capability, and granular reporting that Windows cannot provide.
Will PowerPlug interrupt my end users or disrupt active tasks?
No. PowerPlug detects user activity before applying any power action. If a user is active, the policy action is deferred. Admins can also configure grace-period notifications so users are aware before a scheduled shutdown.
How long does deployment typically take?
Most organisations are fully deployed and seeing measurable savings within days to a few weeks, depending on endpoint count and environment complexity. PowerPlug integrates directly with Active Directory and SCCM to streamline rollout.
What is the WakeUp Portal and who uses it?
The WakeUp Portal is a self-service web interface that lets end users remotely power on their office PC — without IT involvement. It is especially useful for hybrid and remote workers who need to access files or applications on their office machine.
Does PowerPlug support organisations with remote or hybrid workers?
Yes. The WakeUp technology is designed specifically for this scenario. Remote employees can wake their office PC on demand and connect to it via VPN or remote desktop — eliminating the need to leave machines powered on 24/7 for remote access.
How does PowerPlug contribute to sustainability and ESG goals?
Reducing PC energy consumption directly lowers your organisation's carbon footprint. PowerPlug provides energy savings reports that can be used in ESG disclosures, green procurement documentation, and corporate sustainability reporting — as demonstrated by Ben Gurion University's green campus initiative.
