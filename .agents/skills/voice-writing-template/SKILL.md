# Voice Writing Style Template

## Purpose
Template for capturing your authentic writing voice. 7 sections that encode the gap between what AI drafts and what you actually send — the most important signal for voice calibration.

## When To Use
- You keep rewriting AI drafts before sending
- Your AI-generated content sounds generic or corporate
- Building a personal brand through writing
- Managing communications across multiple channels (LinkedIn, HN, email, internal)

## How To Apply
**Fill in 7 sections:**
1. **Voice**: How you actually write (not ideally — actually)
2. **Core Rules**: Your non-negotiables from your own past writing
3. **Anti-Filler Checklist**: Patterns you always delete from AI drafts
4. **Audience Adaptation**: Different registers (internal/external, technical/business)
5. **Channel-Specific Notes**: What changes per platform (LinkedIn vs HN vs email)
6. **Drafted vs Sent**: Real examples of AI drafts vs what you actually sent + lessons ← **MOST IMPORTANT**
7. **Company Context**: How you talk about product, competitors, positioning

**Calibration process:**
1. Read 10-20 things you wrote by hand without AI
2. Look for what's consistent
3. Fill in each section with specific patterns (not "write clearly" — too vague)
4. Add Drafted vs Sent examples every time you rewrite an AI draft

## Examples
- "Write a LinkedIn post about our new product launch in my voice" → uses filled voice template
- "Draft a cold email to this prospect" → applies channel-specific + voice rules

## Integration Notes
- Drop filled template into Claude as a skill or system prompt
- Living document: keep calibrating as your voice evolves
- Anti-filler > style rules: the gap between draft and sent is more actionable than style descriptions
